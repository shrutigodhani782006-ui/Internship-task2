Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

The Missile Man of India and the People's President

Why He is a Legend

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam is remembered as one of India's most loved leaders and scientists. Coming from a humble background in Rameswaram, his journey to becoming the President of India is an inspiration to millions. He played a pivotal role in the development of India's civilian space program and military missile development.

What I adore most about him is his simplicity and his unwavering belief in the power of youth. Even after his presidency, he spent his time traveling across the country to interact with students, encouraging them to "dream big."

His contribution to India's defense through the Integrated Guided Missile Development Program (IGMDP) gave India the strength to stand tall on the global stage. He was also a prolific writer, with books like "Wings of Fire" and "Ignited Minds" continuing to inspire generations.

Time line of a Great Soul:

  • 1931 - Born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu.
  • 1954 - Graduated in Physics from Saint Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli.
  • 1960 - Joined the Aeronautical Development Establishment of the DRDO.
  • 1969 - Transferred to ISRO where he was the project director of India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III).
  • 1998 - Served as the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister and played a key role in the Pokhran-II nuclear tests.
  • 2002 - Elected as the 11th President of India.
  • 2015 - Passed away while doing what he loved most: delivering a lecture to students at IIM Shillong.
